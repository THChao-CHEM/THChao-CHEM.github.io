About

I am a PhD researcher working at the intersection of computational chemistry, materials design, biomolecular machine learning, and mechanochemistry. My work focuses on using models and molecular insight to accelerate experimental decision-making and reduce search space in complex chemical systems.

This website highlights research that translates well to industrial environments: predictive modeling, scalable workflows, interpretable analysis, high throughput screening, and rigorous communication of technical results.

Selected Work

Case Study 01

Predicting Intrinsically Disordered Proteins with Polymer Physics

I investigated how molecular structure influences material properties in plastic crystal systems using molecular dynamics simulations and statistical analysis. The goal was to identify the most informative descriptors for accurate and interpretable physical property prediction.

Methods: Physics-informed feature engineering, machine learning, GPU training, parallel computing. Outcome: Processed 10,000+ protein sequences in parallel, trained models on millions of protein sequences, and improved scalability and predictive performance.

Case Study 02

Modeling Structure-Property Relationships in Plastic Crystals

I investigated how molecular structure influences material properties in plastic crystal systems using molecular dynamics simulations and statistical analysis. The goal was to identify the most informative descriptors for accurate and interpretable physical property prediction.

Methods: Molecular dynamics simulations, statistical analysis, feature engineering, combinatorial feature selection Outcome: Identified optimal feature sets for property prediction and built a more systematic framework for understanding structure-property relationships in plastic crystal materials.

Case Study 03

High-Throughput Discovery of Mechano-Accessible Organic Reactions

I developed computational workflows to predict mechano-accessible organic reactions at scale. This work combined machine-learned interatomic potentials with transition-state search methods to accelerate reaction screening and discovery.

Methods: Machine-learned interatomic potentials, transition-state search algorithms, high-throughput screening, parallel computing Outcome: Built a screening pipeline covering 38,000 organic reactions, achieved a transition-state identification success rate above 70%, and improved workflow efficiency by more than 50%.
